# 50 Career Quotes on Building, Proving & Narrating Your Work

---

## Publishing Your Own Work

1. "Make stuff you love and talk about stuff you love and you'll attract people who love that kind of stuff. It's that simple."
   — **Austin Kleon**, *Show Your Work!*

2. "Once a day, after you've done your day's work, go back to your documentation and find one little piece of your process that you can share."
   — **Austin Kleon**, *Show Your Work!*

3. "The only way to find your voice is to use it. It's hardwired, built into you. Talk about the things you love. Your voice will follow."
   — **Austin Kleon**, *Show Your Work!*

4. "Ideas in secret die. They need light and air or they starve to death."
   — **Seth Godin**

5. "Are you a serial idea-starting person? The goal is to be an idea-shipping person."
   — **Seth Godin**

6. "One of the best ways to build your reputation is by creating content that allows your ideas to travel beyond your immediate circle."
   — **Dorie Clark**, *Stand Out*

7. "In a crowded marketplace, fitting in is failing. In a busy marketplace, not standing out is the same as being invisible."
   — **Seth Godin**

## Thinking About Work

8. "Spend 10% of your week thinking about the work, not just doing the work."
   — *Unattributed*

9. "Most people work *in* their career. Very few work *on* their career. That's the gap."
   — *Unattributed*

10. "I want to spend time on what's important, instead of what's immediate."
    — **Cal Newport**, *So Good They Can't Ignore You*

11. "Playing the long game — eschewing short-term gratification in order to work toward an uncertain but worthy future goal — isn't easy. But it's the surest path to meaningful and lasting success."
    — **Dorie Clark**, *The Long Game*

12. "In today's competitive economy, it's not enough to simply do your job well. Developing a reputation as an expert in your field attracts people who want to hire you, do business with you, and spread your ideas."
    — **Dorie Clark**, *Stand Out*

13. "Guard your time. Forget the money."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

## Narrating Your Own Story

14. "Everybody loves a good story, but good storytelling doesn't come easy to everybody. It's a skill that takes a lifetime to master. So study the great stories and then go find some of your own."
    — **Austin Kleon**, *Show Your Work!*

15. "Human beings want to know where things came from, how they were made, and who made them."
    — **Austin Kleon**, *Show Your Work!*

16. "If you don't tell your story, someone else will — and they'll get it wrong."
    — *Unattributed* (common career coaching wisdom)

17. "Marketing is the generous act of helping others become who they seek to become. It involves creating honest stories — stories that resonate and spread."
    — **Seth Godin**, *This Is Marketing*

18. "Your tactics can make a difference, but your strategy — your commitment to a way of being and a story to be told and a promise to be made — can change everything."
    — **Seth Godin**, *This Is Marketing*

19. "If you don't design your own life plan, chances are you'll fall into someone else's plan. And guess what they have planned for you? Not much."
    — **Jim Rohn**

20. "A career without a narrative is just a list of jobs. A career with a narrative is a trajectory."
    — *Unattributed*

## Building That Perception

21. "The brand is a story. But it's a story about you, not about the brand."
    — **Seth Godin**

22. "You can't build a reputation on what you're going to do."
    — **Confucius** (also often cited by **Abraham Lincoln**)

23. "You can't be seen until you learn to see."
    — **Seth Godin**, *This Is Marketing*

24. "Expectations are the engines of our perceptions."
    — **Seth Godin**

25. "The people who have the ability to fail in public under their own name actually gain a lot of power."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

26. "Embrace accountability and take business risks under your own name. Society will reward you with responsibility, equity, and leverage."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

27. "Persistent, consistent, and frequent stories, delivered to an aligned audience, will earn attention, trust, and action."
    — **Seth Godin**, *This Is Marketing*

28. "Those who work hard and constantly seek to be visible to their superiors, those who showcase their hard work, are the ones who advance to positions of greater power and responsibility."
    — **Abhishek Ratna**, *No Parking. No Halt. Success Non Stop!*

## Building Career Capital

29. "If your goal is to love what you do, you must first build up 'career capital' by mastering rare and valuable skills, and then cash in this capital for the traits that define great work."
    — **Cal Newport**, *So Good They Can't Ignore You*

30. "Don't follow your passion; rather, let it follow you in your quest to become, in the words of my favorite Steve Martin quote, 'so good that they can't ignore you.'"
    — **Cal Newport**, *So Good They Can't Ignore You*

31. "If you want to love what you do, abandon the passion mindset ('what can the world offer me?') and instead adopt the craftsman mindset ('what can I offer the world?')."
    — **Cal Newport**, *So Good They Can't Ignore You*

32. "All returns in life, whether in wealth, relationships, or knowledge, come from compound interest."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

33. "Become the best in the world at what you do. Keep redefining what you do until this is true."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

34. "We live in an age of infinite leverage, and the economic rewards for genuine intellectual curiosity have never been higher."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

35. "What I've come to love about patience is that, ultimately, it's the truest test of merit: Are you willing to do the work, despite no guaranteed outcome?"
    — **Dorie Clark**, *The Long Game*

## Having Evidence for Everything

36. "If you don't remember everything important you did, your manager — no matter how great they are — probably doesn't either."
    — **Julia Evans**, *Get your work recognized: write a brag document*

37. "Instead of trying to remember everything you did with your brain, maintain a 'brag document' that lists everything so you can refer to it when you get to performance review season."
    — **Julia Evans**, *Get your work recognized: write a brag document*

38. "It's really easy to lose track of what skills you're learning, and usually when I reflect on this I realize I learned a lot more than I thought."
    — **Julia Evans**, *Get your work recognized: write a brag document*

39. "Don't do invisible work."
    — **Chris Albon**, Director of ML at Wikimedia (also cited by **Gergely Orosz**, *The Pragmatic Engineer*)

40. "The discipline of writing something down is the first step toward making it happen."
    — **Lee Iacocca**

41. "An accomplishment without evidence is just a claim. An accomplishment with evidence is a case."
    — *Unattributed*

## Building the Skill Repository

42. "Specific knowledge is found by pursuing your innate talents, your genuine curiosity, and your passion. It's not by going to school for whatever is the hottest job."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

43. "Following your genuine intellectual curiosity is a better foundation for a career than following whatever is making money right now."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

44. "Learn to sell. Learn to build. If you can do both, you will be unstoppable."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

45. "If you just show up and work hard, you'll soon hit a performance plateau beyond which you fail to get any better."
    — **Cal Newport**, *So Good They Can't Ignore You* (citing K. Anders Ericsson's research on deliberate practice)

46. "The most important skill for getting rich is becoming a perpetual learner. You have to know how to learn anything you want to learn."
    — **Naval Ravikant**

## Proving Your Progression

47. "Compelling careers often have complex origins that reject the simple idea that all you have to do is follow your passion."
    — **Cal Newport**, *So Good They Can't Ignore You*

48. "The people who get what they're after are very often the ones who just stick around long enough."
    — **Austin Kleon**, *Show Your Work!*

49. "Careers are a jungle gym, not a ladder."
    — **Sheryl Sandberg**, *Lean In*

50. "20% of one's time is not going to bankrupt anybody… if it actually turns out that that 20% is allocated to something that is interesting, it could be a thing. A few months or years on it, all of a sudden, you have built up an amazing side career, an amazing new direction."
    — **Dorie Clark**, *The Long Game*

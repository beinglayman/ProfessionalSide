Led OAuth2 Migration Across 3 Microservices

Jan 15 – Feb 28, 2026 4 participants 7 evidence sources
S

Situation

Our authentication system relied on a legacy session-based approach that couldn't scale across our growing microservice architecture. Three critical services — user-auth, payment-gateway, and notification-hub — each maintained separate session stores, causing inconsistent login states and a 12% session-drop rate.

T

Task

I was tasked with migrating all three services to OAuth2 with JWT tokens, establishing a centralized identity provider, and ensuring zero-downtime during the transition. The migration needed to complete within Q1 2026 while maintaining backward compatibility.

A

Action

I designed a phased rollout strategy starting with the user-auth service as the identity provider. I implemented OAuth2 authorization code flow with PKCE, set up JWT token rotation, and created a compatibility shim that allowed legacy sessions to coexist during migration. I coordinated code reviews across all three service teams and ran load testing at 3x production traffic.

R

Result

The migration reduced session-related incidents by 94%, eliminated the session-drop issue entirely, and improved auth response times from 340ms to 45ms. The centralized identity provider now serves 12 services beyond the original three.
